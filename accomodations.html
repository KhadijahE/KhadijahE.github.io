Staying in Iceland

Photos by Khadijah Erskine

Accommodations in Iceland range from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Whether you are on a budget or have a large group, there is going to be something for you. I personally went with an Airbnb in Reykjavik because of the location, privacy, convenience, and long-stay discount. It was easy to have a central location and drive to all the sights we wanted to see. Reykjavik, the capital city, has the largest selection of accommodations in Iceland, including hotels, hostels, guesthouses, and apartments. There are also many options for accommodations outside of Reykjavik, including in rural areas and small towns.
Here are some popular types of accommodations in Iceland:

Hotels:
Iceland has a wide range of hotels, from budget-friendly options to luxury hotels. Many hotels offer amenities such as hot tubs, saunas, and spa services, and some have restaurants and bars.
Airbnb:
Airbnb is a popular online platform that connects travelers with local hosts who rent out their homes, apartments, or rooms. Staying in an Airbnb can offer a more authentic and local experience,
Hostels:
Iceland has many hostels, which are a great option for solo travelers or groups on a budget. Hostels offer dormitory-style rooms, often with shared bathrooms and communal areas such as kitchens and lounges.
Camping:
Camping is a popular way to experience Iceland's natural beauty, with campsites located throughout the country. Some campsites offer amenities such as hot tubs and laundry facilities. This option take the most planning expecially with Iceland's weather and climate.
Unique lodging options:
Iceland offers a range of unique lodging options,tents, houses, and even stays in camper vans.
