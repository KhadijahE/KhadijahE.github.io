Food in Iceland

Photos by Khadijah Erskine

Icelandic cuisine is typically based on ingredients found in the local environment, such as seafood, lamb, dairy products, and wild berries. Traditional Icelandic dishes include fermented shark, smoked lamb, fish stew, and skyr (a type of cultured yogurt). Other popular foods in Iceland include hot dogs, pastries, and various types of seafood, such as cod and haddock. Whether you are on a budget or want fine dining options, there is a place for you to eat delicious food in Iceland!

Foodhalls and Markets

Photo by Khadijah Erskine

Food halls, also known as market halls or food courts, are popular dining destinations in Iceland. These indoor marketplaces offer a variety of food options, ranging from traditional Icelandic dishes to international cuisine. Some of the most popular food halls in Iceland include:

Hlemmur Mathöll:
Another popular food hall in Reykjavik, Hlemmur Mathöll offers a wide selection of food stalls, including traditional Icelandic dishes, sushi, and vegetarian options.
Grandi Mathöll:
This modern food hall in Reykjavik features a variety of food stalls and a cozy atmosphere. It is a great place to sample a variety of Icelandic dishes and international cuisine.
Kolaportið:
Located in Reykjavik, this is Iceland's largest indoor flea market and offers a variety of food stalls, including seafood, smoked lamb, and traditional sweets. There are also plenty of shops to get souvenirs and gifts! This market is only open on weekends so make sure to stop by when you can!
Bæjarins Beztu Pylsur:
Although this is not technically a food hall, this famous hot dog stand in Reykjavik is a must-visit for anyone who loves street food. It's been serving up traditional Icelandic hot dogs for decades and is a popular spot for locals and tourists alike.

My favorite was Hlemmur Mathöll because it was conveniently located 10 minutes away from my AirBnb. They also had plenty of space for me to study and eat delicious food. Food halls are a great way to taste a variety of Icelandic food in one place, and they offer a unique and convenient dining experience.

Restaurants

Photo by Khadijah Erskine

Iceland has a diverse food scene, with a range of restaurants and bakeries offering both traditional Icelandic dishes and international cuisine. Reykjavik, the capital city, has the largest concentration of restaurants, cafes, and bakeries, with many options for vegetarian and vegan diets. Seafood restaurants are particularly popular, as Iceland is known for its fresh fish. Traditional bakeries can be found throughout the country, offering fresh bread and pastries. There are some recognizable chains include Subway and Domino's Pizza but if you are looking for more traditional restaurants as well as coffee shops like Te og Kaffi and Kaffitár. Fine dining options are also available, with some restaurants featuring innovative menus using locally sourced ingredients. I’ve listed some great restaurants to choose from!

Grocery Stores

Grocery stores in Iceland, provide citizens and visitors with a variety of food and household items. Although Iceland is notorious for high prices, grocery stores provide an extremely affordable option to stock up on essentials. Reykjavik, being the largest city and capital of Iceland, has a number of large chain supermarkets and smaller, locally-owned stores to choose from. Whether you are shopping for ingredients for a home-cooked meal or for everyday essentials, grocery stores in Reykjavik offer a wide selection of both locally-sourced and imported products. I made sure to get groceries so I could make lunch and breakfast on certain days instead of eating at restaurants. Just make sure to check the opening hours of grocery stores as some have limited hours in comparison to what you may be used to in the United States.

Bónus
Bónus is probably the most popular supermarket chain in Iceland. Known for its low prices and wide range of products, Bónus stores are recognizable by their distinctive yellow and blue branding and pink piggy bank as its symbol. There are 31 stores in Iceland and they can be found throughout Reykjavik and the rest of the country. Bónus offers a wide selection of products, including fresh produce, meat, dairy, and household items.
Krónan
Krónan is another popular grocery store chain in Iceland. The store offers a wide variety of food products, including fresh produce, meats, dairy, bakery items, and packaged goods. You can also find household and personal care items. When I could not find what I wanted at Bónus I could find it here!
