Safety First

Photos by Khadijah Erskine

Iceland is considered one of the safest countries in the world with an extremely low crime rate. Icelandâ€™s environment and weather still pose dangerous conditions if you are not careful. No matter what, you should still take some precautions to ensure your safety and wellbeing. Some safety tips for visitors to Iceland include:

  • Always check the weather and road conditions before going on any outdoor activities or driving. The weather in Iceland can be unpredictable, and conditions on the roads can change rapidly, especially during the winter months.
  • Follow all safety guidelines and warnings at natural attractions, such as waterfalls. These areas can be slippery and dangerous if not treated with caution and respect.
  • When hiking, make sure to tell someone your plans and expected return time. I recommend a GPS device or map, along with appropriate clothing and equipment.
  • Be cautious when swimming in the ocean or around waterfalls, as the currents and water temperatures can be dangerous. Avoid going close to water at places like Reynisfjara beach due to deadly sneaker waves.
  • Avoid leaving valuables or personal belongings visible in cars and always lock your doors.
Overall, I believe as long as you take the necessary precautions you will have a safe and enjoyable stay. I have felt the safest in Iceland in all my travels!
